[Web Redesigns]

Williams College Study Abroad Web Redesign

Our team was tasked with redesigning the Williams Global Education and Study Away website for a mobile phone format, with specific focus on tailoring the design for use by international students. The Williams Global Education and Study Away website provides individual study away program details as well as general information on advising, finances, and the application process. The current homepage of the Global Education and Study Away website features buttons that link to Program Options, Winter Study, WEPO, and Williams-Mystic. The major sections our team imagined would be most useful to display on the homepage would differ from these when considering our target audience. Our design was both informed by general design principles focused on maximizing clarity and ease of navigation as well as emphasizing elements that would be particularly useful or of interest to international users.

[Gesture Recognition]

Gesture Recognition for Touch-Free Gaming Experiences

Touch-free computer interactions have existed since the late 20th century. In their earliest form, voice command technologies could only recognize spoken numbers. Eye-tracking interfaces were similarly limited, allowing users to move the cursor with their eyes but offering little control beyond that. However, in 1985, Myron Krueger introduced the first gesture-controlled interaction, enabling users to manipulate computer graphics through body movements.

In our design sprint, we explored Krueger’s innovation using modern technology. Our goal was to translate hand gesture inputs into keyboard control outputs. Essentially, we aimed to play our favorite childhood games using only hand gestures.

[Browser Extension]

EphWrite

Regardless if you’re a high school student, college professor, journalist, or just an average Joe, you’ve probably had to a research paper at some point in your life. We’ve all been there, 20 tabs open, chasing sources as you go down a rabbit hole of discovery. Keeping track of all our ideas, connections, and summaries can be difficult to organize as you search deepens, it’s easy to lose track of brilliant insights along the way.

EphWrite help you organize your thoughts as you navigate through various websites, saving your notes to each website as you search. Our serves to improve user experiences online during the researching and writing of a paper. We allow the user to endlessly cite any and all website they visit as well as write notes for website specific notes. This organization would remind the user of the importances of each of the webpages as they revisit the website. This would save and effort for the user once they revisit websites to write their paper.
